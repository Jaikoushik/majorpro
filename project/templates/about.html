What are Dark Patterns?

Dark patterns are deceptive user interface designs crafted to trick users into doing things they wouldn't otherwise choose to do, often against their best interests.

These manipulative design techniques exploit cognitive biases and psychological vulnerabilities to influence user behavior in ways that benefit the business at the expense of the user's time, money, attention, or privacy.

Common Dark Patterns in E-commerce:
  • Fake Urgency: Countdown timers or "limited time" offers that aren't actually limited
  • Fake Scarcity: "Only 2 left in stock!" messages that may not be accurate
  • Misleading Pricing: Artificially inflated "original" prices to make discounts seem larger
  • Hidden Costs: Adding unexpected fees during checkout
  • Forced Continuity: Making it difficult to cancel subscriptions
  • Misleading Information: Deceptive product descriptions or statistics
About This Tool

The Dark Pattern Detector is a tool designed to help consumers identify potentially manipulative design practices on e-commerce websites and social media platforms.

By leveraging artificial intelligence, specifically Google's Gemini AI model, our tool analyzes product listings and social media content to detect common dark patterns and manipulative techniques.

Key Features:
  • Automated detection of deceptive pricing strategies
  • Identification of artificial urgency and scarcity tactics
  • Analysis of misleading product information
  • Detection of manipulative marketing language
  • History of all previous analyses for reference

Note: This tool is designed for educational and informational purposes. While we strive for accuracy, the detection of dark patterns is subjective and our AI analysis should be used as a starting point for your own critical evaluation.

  • Report suspected dark patterns to consumer protection agencies
  • Support businesses that use transparent and ethical design practices
